Proof strategies revisited

[2020-07-22 Wed]

Prelude

Statements in logic are either true or false. Also, true and false are the negation of each other.

A statement is a (possibly complex) formula of boolean logic.

Definition of proof

In the first chapter of his book How to prove it, Velleman mentions at least three times what defines a valid proof.

A proof is valid if the premises can't be true without the conclusion being true as well.

Meaning of proof

The premises are statements that are given to be true at the beginning of the proof. The conclusion is a statement (constructed from the premises) that must be true at the end of the proof.

Said differently, the conclusion must not be false when the premises are true, for the proof to be valid.

This is useful because once you've established the proof to be valid, you can use it with whatever conditions make the premises true, and be sure that the conclusion is true as well!
